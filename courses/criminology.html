Criminology

The systematic study of crime, criminals, and the societal responses to criminal behavior, seeking to understand and address the complexities of the criminal justice system.

Prof Michael, HOD

Welcome to the intriguing field of criminology, where the study of crime and its various dimensions unveils a nuanced understanding of human behavior, societal structures, and the mechanisms of justice. As your lecturer, I am excited to guide you through a comprehensive exploration of criminology, a discipline that goes beyond the mere examination of criminal acts to delve into the root causes, patterns, and societal reactions to criminal behavior. In this course, we will unravel the complexities of crime by examining the psychological, sociological, and economic factors that contribute to its occurrence. From classical theories to contemporary perspectives, you will gain insights into the motivations behind criminal behavior and the impact of social structures on the prevalence of crime in diverse communities. Our curriculum is designed to foster critical thinking, enabling you to analyze and evaluate the effectiveness of various criminal justice policies and interventions. Furthermore, criminology is not merely an academic pursuit; it is a dynamic field that interfaces with the criminal justice system and broader societal issues. Through case studies, discussions, and real-world applications, you will explore the practical implications of criminological theories and research. We will also delve into ethical considerations in criminological research and policymaking, emphasizing the importance of responsible and informed approaches to address the multifaceted challenges within the realm of crime and justice. Join us on this intellectual journey as we navigate the complexities of criminology, seeking to contribute to a more just and understanding society.

  • Comprehensive Exploration of Criminal Behavior
  • Critical Analysis of Criminal Justice Policies
  • Real-World Applications and Case Studies
